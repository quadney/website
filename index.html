About

I'm ending my third year as a Digital Arts and Sciences major at the University of Florida. Digital Arts and Sciences is in the College of Computer Science and Engineering - this is a major in which design meets computer science, and art meets engineering.

After attending an Art Magnet school in Miami, Design and Architecture Senior High, I grew tired of strictly art, and decided to pursue computer science. This is what attracted me to UF's Digital Arts and Sciences program, it integrates the challenge and problem solving of computer programming, with design and creativity.

I'm interested in Human-Computer Interaction, Program Management, Mobile Development, and Software Engineering in general - in addition, I love teaching others what I know and have learned. I have explored these areas through coursework in school, internships, and learning on my own time. When I have something in mind that I want to learn - I dedicate myself for weeks until I feel confident that I can teach someone else. This summer I have an internship with Amazon in the Transaction Risk Management Services - I'll be "integrating new research" with their current texhnology, in addition, I'll be learning cloud computing, data mining, and machine learning concepts. I'm incredibily excited to be living and working in the Seattle area for three months!

I have a problem: I want to learn everything. Here is the summary of my attempts.

UF Library App

This was the product of my term project for Human Computer Interaction with Benjamin Lok. The assignment was to redesign an interface, and test whether the design was successful or not by implementing a user-study. My idea was to redesign the UF Library's interface that lets students know how many computers are available in a library. This is inefficient - sometimes students do not want to use a computer, and need to know if the library is busy or not before trekking there. Unfortunately, due to problem with accessing UF databases and recorded data, I could not get the right information I needed. I worked around this by asking users to subjectively rate how busy the library was if they were there.

Launch Library App

The Fractal Machine

This was my final project for Introduction to Digital Arts and Sciences with Dave Small in Fall 2012. After creating a character in the program, you can explore different types of fractals with the character as the subject. Fractals include MCRM (multiple copy reducing machine), L-System, Mandelbrot and Julia Sets, and "create a scene" with plant-like fractals. This class was the second-most rewarding class to date - I learned invaluable knowledge to create programs written in Java, and a taste of modeling natural phenomenas in code.
