Java: The Board Game

This was a semester-long project for the class Object Oriented Programming that taught us [the hard way] how to design and write good OO code. The first two iterations, I was in a team of nine other students where we debated about how to design the system, wrote use-cases, design documents, implemented the design, and presented to the class what we had made. For the third iteration, we were split into different groups and had to start fresh. This class was literally like a full time job - super challenging to balance with the other classes I was taking, but also the most fun because of the inside jokes and friendships that were formed. Implementing these have really taught me the value of testing code (truly an inspiration to do Test Driven Development) and the value of writing reuseable code. It was also an inspiration to take Design Patterns in the Fall.

Lecture Loot

Lecture Loot was the term project for Intro to Software Engineering - our goal was to encourage students to attend classes more, buy enticing them to ear “money” to go to class. The idea was to bet on yourself to go to class, and if you did, then you wouldn’t lose any money - at the end of the week, you would get a certain percentage of money from the pool of people who didn’t go to each class that week. I worked on the front-end of Android app initially, and then once that was set up, I worked on the front-end and backend logic for the iOS app. What was most fun about this project was learning how to write Android and iOS apps - they each have their quirks that are really interesting to deal with.

Papercuts

Papercuts is a video game created with three other students (team DigiSquid) in Spring 2013 for Theory and Production of Digital Arts and Sciences with Dave Small. Papercuts was created in the Unity 3D game engine. My role was as Aesthetic Lead - I made decisions and allocated tasks based on design of the 3D models, world layout, and textures.
